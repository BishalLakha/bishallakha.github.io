Hi! I am Bishal, a Ph.D. Candidate in Computer Science at [Boise State University](https://www.boisestate.edu/), advised by [Dr. Edoardo Serra](https://sites.google.com/site/serraedoardo/). My research focuses on developing reliable and scalable Graph ML-based algorithms, primarily for anomaly detection and cybersecurity. I also work on generative models for graph generation, LLMs, and adversarial learning.

I am currently a Ph.D. Intern at the [Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (PNNL)](https://www.pnnl.gov/), contributing to the [PermitAI project](https://www.pnnl.gov/projects/permitai/) with a focus on model benchmarking and LLM customization for NEPA document drafting. Previously, I interned at [Mitsubishi Electric Research Laboratories (MERL)](https://www.merl.com/), where I developed spatio-temporal graph anomaly detection algorithms for IoT networks.

Before graduate school, I worked as a Machine Learning Engineer at [Paaila Technology](https://www.paaila.ai/) and co-founded [Newrun Technology](https://newrun.com.np/), leading projects in computer vision, speech recognition, and AI-driven automation. My undergraduate degree is in Electronics and Communication Engineering from IOE Pulchowk Campus, Tribhuvan University, Nepal.
